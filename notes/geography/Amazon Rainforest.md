# Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest spans nine countries and covers 5.5 million square kilometers, holding roughly 10% of all species on Earth and 390 billion individual trees. It is a living engine of [[Photosynthesis]], cycling water and carbon on a planetary scale: the forest generates much of its own rainfall, exhaling moisture that falls again as rain thousands of kilometers away.

Its biodiversity is a genetic library — the [[DNA]] of its plants has yielded medicines from muscle relaxants to cancer treatments, with countless compounds still unstudied. Indigenous peoples have stewarded the forest for over 10,000 years.

The Amazon River discharges more water than the next seven largest rivers combined, exceeding even the [[Nile River]] in volume by an enormous margin. Deforestation, driven by agriculture and pressures dating back to the [[Industrial Revolution]]'s appetite for resources, threatens to tip the ecosystem into savanna.

Scientists now use satellites and [[Artificial Intelligence]] to detect illegal clearing in near real time.
