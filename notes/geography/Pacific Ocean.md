# Pacific Ocean

The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest body of water on Earth, covering more than 30% of the planet's surface — larger than all land combined. Its deepest point, the Challenger Deep in the Mariana Trench, plunges 10,935 meters; [[Mount Everest]] could sink into it with more than two kilometers of water to spare.

The Pacific's phytoplankton perform a huge share of global [[Photosynthesis]], producing much of the oxygen we breathe. Its "Ring of Fire" hosts 75% of the world's volcanoes and most of its earthquakes, driven by the same tectonic forces that raise mountain ranges.

Tides sweep across it under the pull of [[The Moon]], and its currents — like the vast Pacific gyres — regulate climate worldwide through El Niño and La Niña cycles.

The ocean was the stage for the island-hopping campaigns of [[World War II]], and today its seabed carries the fiber-optic cables that transmit most of [[The Internet]]'s intercontinental traffic.
