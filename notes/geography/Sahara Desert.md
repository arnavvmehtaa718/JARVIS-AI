# Sahara Desert

The Sahara is the largest hot desert on Earth, covering 9.2 million square kilometers of North Africa — nearly the size of the United States. Yet it was not always barren: cave paintings and lake sediments show that as recently as 6,000 years ago the "Green Sahara" held grasslands, hippos, and human settlements before orbital shifts dried it out.

The desert's eastern edge is pierced by the [[Nile River]], the lifeline that made [[Ancient Egypt]] possible amid the sands. During [[World War II]], the Sahara's western reaches saw pivotal tank battles between Allied and Axis forces.

Saharan dust does surprising global work: winds carry about 180 million tonnes of it each year across the Atlantic, fertilizing the [[Amazon Rainforest]] with phosphorus. The desert's relentless sunshine also makes it a prime candidate for vast solar farms — some proposals claim a fraction of its area could power the world with [[Renewable Energy]], though transmission and dust remain hard problems.
