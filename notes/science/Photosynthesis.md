# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight from [[The Sun]] into chemical energy. Using water, carbon dioxide, and light, they produce glucose and release the oxygen that fills our atmosphere and makes animal life possible.

Nearly all the free oxygen on Earth was created this way. The [[Amazon Rainforest]] is often called the planet's lungs, though ocean phytoplankton in the [[Pacific Ocean]] actually generate the majority of atmospheric oxygen.

The molecular machinery of photosynthesis is inherited through [[DNA]] and refined over billions of years of evolution. Chlorophyll, the green pigment that captures light, is remarkably efficient — and quantum effects described by [[Quantum Mechanics]] appear to help funnel energy through the process.

Understanding and mimicking photosynthesis is a major goal of [[Renewable Energy]] research: "artificial leaves" aim to split water and store solar energy as clean fuel.
