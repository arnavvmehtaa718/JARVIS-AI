# Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is the theory describing nature at the smallest scales — atoms, electrons, and photons. It replaced the clockwork certainty of classical physics with probabilities, superposition, and entanglement, where particles can be correlated across vast distances.

Its predictions are the most precisely verified in all of science, underpinning the [[Semiconductors]] inside every computer and the lasers that carry [[The Internet]] across oceans. Without it, the modern [[Periodic Table]] of elements could not be explained, since quantum rules govern how electrons fill atomic shells.

Quantum theory also collides with [[Gravity]] at the edges of [[Black Holes]], where our two great physical frameworks — quantum mechanics and general relativity — refuse to reconcile. Solving that clash is one of the deepest open problems in physics.

Today, quantum computers exploit superposition to tackle problems intractable for classical machines, and researchers race to pair them with [[Artificial Intelligence]] for breakthroughs in chemistry and materials.
