# Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from [[The Sun]], has fascinated humanity since antiquity. Its red color comes from iron oxide — rust — covering its surface. The planet hosts the solar system's tallest volcano, Olympus Mons (21 km high), and a canyon system, Valles Marineris, that would stretch across the entire United States.

Evidence gathered by NASA's rovers shows Mars once had rivers, lakes, and possibly oceans. Today its water is locked in polar ice caps and underground deposits. The question of whether microbial life ever arose there — perhaps leaving traces in its [[DNA]]-like chemistry, if life used similar molecules — drives ongoing missions.

Mars has gravity about 38% of Earth's, a thin carbon dioxide atmosphere, and dust storms that can engulf the planet. SpaceX and NASA both target crewed missions in the coming decades, with [[Artificial Intelligence]] guiding autonomous landings and habitat systems. Compared with [[The Moon]], Mars is a far harder but more rewarding destination.
