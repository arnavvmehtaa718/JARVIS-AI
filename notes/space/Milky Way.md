# Milky Way

The Milky Way is our home galaxy: a barred spiral roughly 100,000 light-years across containing between 100 and 400 billion stars, of which [[The Sun]] is just one unremarkable member. Nearly every star you can see with the naked eye belongs to it.

At its center sits Sagittarius A*, a supermassive black hole four million times the Sun's mass — see [[Black Holes]] for how such objects warp spacetime and challenge both [[Gravity]] and [[Quantum Mechanics]].

The galaxy is on a collision course with Andromeda, its nearest large neighbor. In about 4.5 billion years the two will merge into a single elliptical galaxy sometimes nicknamed "Milkomeda."

Ancient civilizations wove the Milky Way's glowing band into mythology — Egyptian sky lore connected it to the [[Nile River]], seeing the heavens as a mirror of their great waterway. Modern surveys like Gaia have now mapped nearly two billion of its stars with astonishing precision.
