# Black Holes

A black hole is a region of spacetime where [[Gravity]] is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape once it crosses the event horizon. They form when massive stars — far heavier than [[The Sun]] — collapse at the end of their lives.

In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope produced the first direct image of a black hole, the supermassive giant at the heart of galaxy M87. Our own [[Milky Way]] hosts one too: Sagittarius A*, about four million times the mass of the Sun.

Black holes are natural laboratories for [[Quantum Mechanics]] and general relativity. Stephen Hawking showed they slowly evaporate through Hawking radiation, meaning even black holes are not truly eternal.

Recent gravitational-wave observatories like LIGO have detected dozens of black hole mergers, opening an entirely new way of listening to the universe.
