# The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star that contains 99.86% of the mass in our solar system. Every second it fuses roughly 600 million tonnes of hydrogen into helium, releasing the energy that powers [[Photosynthesis]] and nearly every ecosystem on Earth, from the [[Amazon Rainforest]] to the plankton of the [[Pacific Ocean]].

Its surface temperature is about 5,500°C, while the corona — its outer atmosphere — mysteriously reaches millions of degrees. Solar flares and coronal mass ejections can disrupt satellites, power grids, and [[The Internet]] infrastructure on Earth.

The Sun orbits the center of the [[Milky Way]] once every 230 million years. In about five billion years it will swell into a red giant, likely engulfing Mercury and Venus. Solar power, a pillar of [[Renewable Energy]], captures a tiny fraction of the 173,000 terawatts of sunlight that continuously strikes Earth.
