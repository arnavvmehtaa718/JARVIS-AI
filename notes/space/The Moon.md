# The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, formed about 4.5 billion years ago, most likely from debris after a Mars-sized body struck the young Earth. Its gravitational pull drives ocean tides across the [[Pacific Ocean]] and every sea on the planet, and it has slowly stabilized Earth's axial tilt, keeping our climate relatively steady.

Twelve humans walked on the lunar surface between 1969 and 1972 during the Apollo program, a direct product of Cold War rivalry that followed [[World War II]]. The samples they returned reshaped our understanding of the solar system.

Today a new lunar race is underway. NASA's Artemis program, along with missions from China and India, aims to establish a permanent presence at the lunar south pole, where water ice hides in permanently shadowed craters. The Moon is also seen as a stepping stone to [[Mars]] and a testbed for technologies from [[Renewable Energy]] systems to [[Artificial Intelligence]]-driven robotics.
