# Semiconductors

Semiconductors are materials — overwhelmingly silicon — whose electrical conductivity can be precisely controlled, making them the foundation of all modern electronics. The transistor, invented at Bell Labs in 1947, exploited this property and became the most manufactured object in human history: a modern chip packs over 100 billion of them.

Their operation is pure applied [[Quantum Mechanics]]: band gaps, electron tunneling, and doped crystal lattices built from elements chosen straight off the [[Periodic Table]]. Fabrication plants ("fabs") are the most precise factories ever constructed, etching features just a few nanometers wide with ultraviolet light.

Chips route every packet on [[The Internet]], train every [[Artificial Intelligence]] model, manage [[Electricity]] in everything from cars to solar inverters for [[Renewable Energy]], and guide spacecraft to [[Mars]].

Because advanced fabrication is concentrated in a few places — notably Taiwan — semiconductors have become a focus of geopolitics, trade policy, and national security worldwide.
