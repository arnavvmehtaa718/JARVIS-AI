# Renewable Energy

Renewable energy comes from sources that replenish naturally: sunlight, wind, water, and heat from the Earth. Solar and wind are now the cheapest forms of new [[Electricity]] generation in most of the world, a stunning reversal from just fifteen years ago driven by manufacturing scale and better [[Semiconductors]].

Solar panels harvest photons from [[The Sun]] using the photovoltaic effect — the same physics Einstein explained in 1905, rooted in [[Quantum Mechanics]]. Nature perfected its own version billions of years earlier through [[Photosynthesis]].

Hydropower from rivers like the [[Nile River]] remains the largest renewable source globally, while proposals for immense solar farms in the [[Sahara Desert]] hint at the scale of what is possible. The transition aims to unwind the fossil-fuel dependence inherited from the [[Industrial Revolution]] and to slow the climate change threatening glaciers on [[Mount Everest]] and forests like the [[Amazon Rainforest]].

Storage, transmission, and grid intelligence — increasingly powered by [[Artificial Intelligence]] — are the next frontiers.
