# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence is the engineering of systems that perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence: recognizing images, understanding language, planning, and prediction. The field traces its founding to the 1956 Dartmouth workshop, but its intellectual roots reach back to Alan Turing's codebreaking machines of [[World War II]].

Modern AI is dominated by deep learning: neural networks trained on vast datasets using specialized [[Semiconductors]] such as GPUs. Large language models can now write, translate, and reason across domains, while systems like AlphaFold have solved the protein-folding problem central to [[DNA]] biology.

AI is deployed everywhere — filtering content on [[The Internet]], spotting deforestation in the [[Amazon Rainforest]] from orbit, navigating rovers on [[Mars]], and optimizing [[Renewable Energy]] grids and [[Electricity]] demand in real time.

The technology's hunger for computing power and data raises hard questions about energy use, bias, and control. Many researchers consider aligning powerful AI with human values one of the defining challenges of this century.
