# The Internet

The Internet is a global network of networks connecting more than five billion people. It grew from ARPANET, a 1969 U.S. research project designed to link computers over long distances, and was transformed for the public by Tim Berners-Lee's invention of the World Wide Web in 1989.

Physically, the Internet is astonishingly tangible: over 1.4 million kilometers of submarine fiber-optic cables cross the [[Pacific Ocean]] and every other sea, carrying light pulses generated by lasers — devices that exist only because of [[Quantum Mechanics]]. Data centers full of [[Semiconductors]] consume so much power that their [[Electricity]] demand now shapes national [[Renewable Energy]] planning.

The Internet's effect on society is often compared to Gutenberg's printing press during the [[Renaissance]]: an information revolution that redistributed knowledge and power. Today it is also the nervous system of [[Artificial Intelligence]], supplying the data and delivering the services that define the modern digital economy.
