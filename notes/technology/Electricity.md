# Electricity

Electricity — the flow of electric charge — powers virtually every aspect of modern life. Humanity's mastery of it unfolded through the 19th century: Faraday's dynamo showed motion could make current, Edison and Swan lit cities with incandescent bulbs, and the "war of currents" between Edison's DC and Tesla and Westinghouse's AC ended with alternating current electrifying the world.

Widespread electrification was the crowning act of the [[Industrial Revolution]], remaking factories, homes, and nights themselves. Today the global grid is civilization's largest machine, and keeping it stable while integrating [[Renewable Energy]] from sun and wind is a central engineering challenge — increasingly managed with help from [[Artificial Intelligence]].

At the deepest level, electricity is quantum: the behavior of electrons in materials is governed by [[Quantum Mechanics]], and controlling that behavior in silicon gave us [[Semiconductors]]. Even life runs on charge — nerve impulses and the energy factories in cells are electrical phenomena, encoded and maintained through [[DNA]].
