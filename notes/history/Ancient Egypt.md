# Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egypt flourished for over three thousand years along the banks of the [[Nile River]], whose annual floods deposited fertile silt that fed one of history's first great civilizations. The Greek historian Herodotus called Egypt "the gift of the Nile."

The Great Pyramid of Giza, built around 2560 BC for Pharaoh Khufu, remained the tallest human-made structure for nearly 4,000 years. Its precise alignment to the cardinal directions shows sophisticated astronomy — Egyptian priests tracked the stars of the [[Milky Way]] and timed their calendar to the star Sirius.

Egyptians pioneered medicine, mathematics, papyrus writing, and monumental engineering, all without iron tools for most of their history. Hieroglyphs remained unreadable for centuries until the Rosetta Stone unlocked them in 1822.

Egypt was eventually absorbed by the [[Roman Empire]] after Cleopatra's defeat in 30 BC, but its imagery and mysteries have never released their grip on the human imagination, inspiring scholars from antiquity through the [[Renaissance]] to today.
