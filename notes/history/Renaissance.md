# Renaissance

The Renaissance ("rebirth") was the explosion of art, science, and humanism that spread from 14th-century Italy across Europe, ending the medieval era. Wealthy city-states like Florence, enriched by banking and trade, funded geniuses such as Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Brunelleschi.

The movement drew inspiration from rediscovered texts of the [[Roman Empire]] and ancient Greece, many preserved by Islamic scholars. Johannes Gutenberg's printing press (c. 1440) spread ideas faster than ever before — an information revolution comparable to [[The Internet]] in its impact.

Renaissance thinkers laid the groundwork for modern science: Copernicus placed [[The Sun]] at the center of the cosmos, Vesalius mapped human anatomy, and Galileo's telescope revealed moons orbiting Jupiter, challenging the idea that everything circled Earth. Leonardo's notebooks sketched flying machines and anatomical studies centuries ahead of their time.

This fusion of art and empirical inquiry flowed directly into the scientific revolution and, ultimately, the [[Industrial Revolution]].
