# World War II

World War II (1939–1945) was the deadliest conflict in human history, claiming an estimated 70 to 85 million lives across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the [[Pacific Ocean]]. It ended with the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki — weapons born from the physics of [[Quantum Mechanics]] and the equations of Einstein.

The war accelerated technology at a staggering pace: radar, jet engines, penicillin mass production, and the first programmable computers, built at Bletchley Park to crack the Enigma cipher. Alan Turing's codebreaking work there planted seeds that grew into modern computing and [[Artificial Intelligence]].

Campaigns raged from the [[Sahara Desert]], where tank armies clashed at El Alamein, to the islands of the Pacific. The conflict redrew the world map, birthed the United Nations, and launched the Cold War rivalry that would eventually put humans on [[The Moon]].

Its aftermath also transformed [[Electricity]] grids, aviation, and industry, setting the stage for the postwar economic boom.
